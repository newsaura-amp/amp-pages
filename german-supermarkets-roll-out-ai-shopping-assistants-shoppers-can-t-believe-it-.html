German Supermarkets Roll Out AI Shopping Assistants—Shoppers Can’t Believe It!

Published: August 7, 2025

Summary: German supermarkets are revolutionizing the shopping experience with AI-powered smart carts. Shoppers in Cologne are using tablets on carts to scan shopping lists, discover new products, and get personalized suggestions—all without apps or registration. The experience astonishes customers, streamlines grocery runs, and hints at the future of retail automation.

Background & Context

Innovation meets aisles: German grocery giant REWE Group partnered with Israeli startup Catch.ai to introduce AI-powered smart shopping carts at a store in Cologne. These carts feature built-in tablets that act as on-the-go assistants—scanning handwritten lists, recommending products, and suggesting deals, all while preserving shoppers’ anonymity.

How It Works & Why Shoppers Are Amazed

  • No sign-ups, no apps needed: Simply use the tablet on the cart to interact with the AI.
  • Real-time suggestions: The AI recommends items, tracks your list, and offers personalized deals.
  • Frictionless shopping: Customers describe it as “mind-blowing” and “a peek into the future.”

Wider Trends & Retail Impact

AI assistants are reshaping how the world shops. AI-generated referrals now drive over 1,200% more traffic to retail websites. Yet, surveys show that 66% of shoppers are hesitant to trust AI for major decisions, citing data and privacy concerns.

In Germany, the trend is growing fast. REWE’s smart carts are just the beginning—AI-based self-checkouts and voice assistants are also emerging across stores.

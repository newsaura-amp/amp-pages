Who Was Bryan Braman?

Bryan Braman, born on May 4, 1987, in Spokane, Washington, was not your average NFL player. Known for his relentless energy on special teams and his powerful playstyle, Braman went undrafted in 2011 but carved out a successful career in the NFL. He played for the Houston Texans and later made his mark with the Philadelphia Eagles, where he won a Super Bowl in 2018.

Braman rose to viral fame in 2011 when he made a tackle without a helmet during a Texans game — an iconic moment that symbolized his fearless nature. Over the years, he played in 97 games, recorded 56 tackles, 1.5 sacks, and even blocked punts, making him one of the most effective special teamers of his time.

Brian Harman Takes Command of The Open: British Open 2025 Leaderboard Update

Royal Portrush, July 18, 2025

The 153rd edition of The Open Championship is heating up, and once again, the name Brian Harman is rising to the top of the British Open 2025 leaderboard. The American golfer, known for his deliberate pre-shot routine and deadly short game, is delivering a repeat-worthy performance at the historic Royal Portrush in Northern Ireland.

Fans of The Open know drama is part of the package. But what makes this year's contest special is the reemergence of Harman, the 2023 Champion Golfer of the Year, who is proving that his win at Hoylake was no fluke. As the second round concludes, Harman holds the solo lead at -7, thanks to a mix of clinical iron play, rock-solid putting, and strategic calm.

Brian Harman: The Rise of a Reluctant Star

While not a household name like Rory McIlroy or Jon Rahm, Brian Harman has built a reputation as one of the most consistent players on the PGA Tour. The left-handed Georgian stunned the golf world in 2023 by dominating The Open at Royal Liverpool. Fast forward to 2025, and he’s once again in the spotlight.

Harman's ability to control his ball flight in windy conditions and sink clutch putts under pressure has made him a formidable force on links courses. His pre-shot routine—a waggle-heavy, methodical setup—has drawn criticism from fans and commentators alike, but the results speak for themselves.

“He's doing everything right,” said former pro Paul McGinley on Sky Sports. “This isn't a fluke. Harman knows how to play Open golf.”

How Harman Seized the Lead

Round 2 started calmly for Harman, who immediately found his rhythm with birdies at holes 1 and 4. His wedge game was pinpoint accurate, leaving several short putts that he converted with ease. By the time he reached the back nine, he was at -6 and tied with Harris English and Rasmus Højgaard. Then came a birdie on the par-4 13th, where he stuffed an approach inside six feet.

Harman's course management has been a masterclass. While other players took aggressive lines, he opted for safe, strategic plays, often laying back from trouble and trusting his short game.

"The rough is brutal this week," Harman said in a post-round interview. "You've got to be smart. I'm not chasing pins—I'm chasing opportunities."
